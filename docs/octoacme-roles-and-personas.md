# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads own quality strategy, test planning, and acceptance validation. They collaborate with developers and product managers to ensure features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards before release.

### Responsibilities
- Design test strategy and test plans for each release
- Define and refine acceptance criteria with Product Manager and developers
- Execute and manage test automation, regression testing, and manual QA
- Validate that features meet acceptance criteria before promotion to production
- Report quality metrics and test coverage to the team
- Identify quality risks and propose mitigations
- Escalate blockers or quality concerns to the PM and Technical Lead
- Establish and maintain Definition of Done (DoD) quality standards

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Maintain high test coverage and automation standards
- Deliver confident releases with clear quality sign-off
- Support rapid iteration without sacrificing quality

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement
- Test result summaries and quality reports
- Defect logs and risk assessments
- Pre-release quality sign-offs
- QA status in weekly execution and tracking sync

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test automation, test data setup, and acceptance criteria clarity
- **Product Managers**: Align on acceptance criteria, user scenarios, and success metrics
- **Technical Lead**: Coordinate on testability, test infrastructure, and technical quality gates
- **Project Manager**: Report quality status and escalate blockers

---

## Technical Lead

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide architectural guidance, conduct design reviews, and assess technical risks. They mentor developers, drive technical decisions, and ensure code quality and system health.

### Responsibilities
- Review technical designs and propose architectural patterns
- Conduct code reviews and mentor developers on best practices
- Identify technical risks, dependencies, and integration points
- Make go/no-go decisions on technical approaches
- Collaborate with PM and QA Lead on testability and quality gates
- Drive technical debt reduction and performance optimization
- Support incident triage and production troubleshooting
- Mentor junior developers and promote knowledge sharing

### Goals
- Ensure scalable, maintainable, and secure architecture
- Reduce technical risk and unplanned work
- Promote knowledge sharing and engineering excellence
- Enable fast, confident deployments

### Typical Communication
- Design review meetings and architecture discussions
- Technical risk registers
- Code review comments and pull request feedback
- Post-incident retrospectives
- Technical guidance in sprint planning

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Mentor, review designs, and provide architectural guidance
- **QA Lead**: Collaborate on testability, test infrastructure, and quality gates
- **Project Manager**: Escalate technical risks and dependencies
- **Stakeholders/Sponsors**: Communicate technical feasibility and trade-offs

---

## Stakeholder/Sponsor

### Role Summary
Sponsors represent business, executive, or customer interests. They provide strategic direction, approve major decisions, and ensure project alignment with organizational goals.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives and success criteria
- Approve scope changes and trade-offs
- Provide escalation authority and decision-making on conflicts
- Receive monthly or milestone-based status updates
- Support resource allocation and removal of organizational blockers
- Sign off on releases and major milestones
- Champion the project and secure organizational commitment

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver measurable business value
- Maintain alignment with organizational strategy
- Reduce scope creep and project delays
- Ensure customer satisfaction and market readiness

### Typical Communication
- Monthly executive status updates
- Milestone or phase sign-offs
- Ad-hoc escalations for major decisions or blockers
- Release announcements and customer communications
- Quarterly business reviews on project ROI

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Receive regular status updates and escalations
- **Product Manager**: Align on roadmap, priorities, and success metrics
- **Entire Team**: Communicate vision, constraints, and business context

---

## Scrum Master/Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, coach teams on agile practices, and help remove impediments. They focus on process health and team velocity.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Coach team on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Track and resolve impediments and blockers
- Maintain sprint boards and artifact transparency
- Support the PM in backlog grooming and iteration planning
- Identify and escalate process inefficiencies
- Mentor team members on agile mindset and collaboration

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and predictability
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Reduce meeting waste and increase transparency
- Enable sustainable, high-performing teams

### Typical Communication
- Sprint ceremonies (standups, planning, reviews, retros)
- Sprint metrics and retrospective action items
- Impediment logs and escalation summaries
- Process improvement recommendations

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Collaborate on schedule and impediment resolution
- **Product Manager**: Support backlog refinement and prioritization
- **Entire Team**: Facilitate ceremonies and coach on agile practices
